What I got wrong (v1)

My original mental model had Dzika as a straight north-south street with the clinic directly above Dzika 19. I drew the 90° corner as ╝ (opening west). Both were wrong:

  • Dzika is a boomerang/V shape - E-W tertiary road on the west, bending into a long SE diagonal on the east
  • The corner opens east (╚ shape), not west
  • Dzika 19 sits at the apex of the bend, not on a straight N-S segment
  • The clinic IS directly north of D19 (same longitude, 156m) - but my description "walk south on Dzika" was misleading because Dzika doesn't go straight north from D19 toward the clinic

Open questions about Dzika

  • Walking route: Google says 78m/1min from D19 to clinic "via Dzika." But Dzika doesn't go straight north. Is there a footpath/sidewalk cutting north from D19 to Słomińskiego? Or does Marta walk along the SE diagonal part of Dzika and then turn north on another street?
  • Best stopping spot: Is D19 (at the bend) the right place? Or should I look at the SE diagonal part which seems to be a calm residential street with parking?
  • Which side of the street? Several segments are one-way. Where would I be driving from, and which curb would I stop at?

5. Technical Notes

How I navigate Street View

Chrome DevTools MCP can take screenshots and click elements, but can't walk in Street View (arrow keys and canvas clicks don't work). My workaround:

  1. Search for a place/address → click "Street View" button on place card → get panorama ID
  2. Change heading by modifying the URL parameter (0°=N, 90°=E, 180°=S, 270°=W)
  3. To "move", search for a nearby address and repeat

This means I can only view SV at locations where Google has searchable places. I can't freely explore between them.

Data sources used

  • Google Maps Street View - via Chrome DevTools MCP (panorama screenshots)
  • Google Maps - satellite imagery, place search, walking directions
  • OpenStreetMap Overpass API - street geometry (coordinates of all road segments)
  • OSM Nominatim - geocoding (tried first, returned incomplete data)

Alternative explored but not used

  • Mapillary - crowd-sourced street-level imagery (Meta). Dense coverage in Warsaw. Has a Graph API for programmatic access. I tried it but my access token was invalid. Could be useful for seeing recent street conditions, parking signs, etc.
  • Apple Maps Look Around - available in Warsaw via beta.maps.apple.com. The binoculars button didn't appear in my browser session.
